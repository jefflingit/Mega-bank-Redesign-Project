CI Report/Summary

users' behavior to bring new insights into User Experience on online foreign exchange system.

Demographics

A total of four respondents, aged 20-55, were interviewed, one with online money exchange experience and four with physical money exchange experience.Two of the younger respondents had more experience with online financial services.

Goals and motivations

The four respondents were asked to perform the same task: starting from the online home page of Mega Bank and using its service to transfer 500,000 KRW online for travel purposes. We observed the possible obstacles and pain points of the respondents in the process of operation, analyzed and optimized them, and hoped to use them as a reference to optimize the user experience of the online remittance system of the website.

Observations/Pain points

1. Navigation/labeling system

All four respondents encountered obstacles when trying to navigate from the home page to the "Online Checkout" function, especially because they were not clear about the meaning of the labels and the information was too complicated, so they spent more time deciphering the information and finding the correct path. Some respondents also clicked on the next step without reading the consent terms or checking the consent box before entering the data.


2. Page functionality/message clarity

Most of the respondents indicated that they were unable to select the branch that best met their needs in the first place due to the lack of geographical information, and could only choose based on their own administrative district. In addition, respondents also said that the drop-down menu for currency conversion did not allow them to immediately understand the logic of the operation (probably because they did not know the value of the currency) and that they needed to make repeated mistakes before they could choose the correct amount. The lack of clear instructions for filling in the basic fields also caused most respondents to make mistakes when filling in the fields for the first time, and the system did not record the original information.


3. Relevant content

Some respondents said that the arrangement of the pages made it easy for them to overlook or even miss important information related to their rights and interests, such as the documents they should bring when they go to exchange foreign currency and the time to pay and collect foreign currency. Due to the incomplete information, respondents may consider using other banks' services. In addition, these conditions existed even after entering the settlement page and were mixed with non-essential information, such as insurance needs and foreign language courses.


Conclusion/Insights

1. Navigation Efficiency:

The label naming of financial services affects the efficiency of the navigation system: For example, inexperienced users may not be able to distinguish between terms such as online settlement, foreign currency trading, cash purchase, etc., and may be better served by personalizing the service rather than naming it objectively


2. Customized Information::

Personalized fill-in fields need more support functions and should not be pre-determined for users to know all the information. For example, users need more information about the geographical location of branches, the drop-down menu for exchange units could have more explanations, and open fields for personal information should have more fill-in support.


3. Layout Readability:

The arrangement of important information on the pages needs to be clearer and more precise. For example, flowcharts should be used to inform users in advance of the cautions for online settlement and repeated on each page to remind users of the corresponding services, and the arrangement of the pages should be such that users can read and pay attention to the information naturally.

Interviewee 1 - Mr. Wu

  • 23-year-old male, with university education.
  • Familiar with foreign exchange and other financial products
  • Has not used online foreign exchange services, and always goes to physical exchange when he needs to exchange.
  • For the selection criteria of the bank, the main consideration is the convenience and stability of the transaction.

Feedback: The service is not suitable for people who do not have much experience in exchanging currency, and the label names on the navigation list cannot tell which is the service they need.

Suggestions: Interviewee felt that the biggest problem with this page was that the information was not clearly explained, and that it should either use easier-to-understand words or provide relevant explanations on the operation page.

Interviewee 2 - Ms. Hong

  • 53-year-old female, with a university education and a career as a civil servant.
  • Not particularly familiar with online system operation.
  • Internet banking related services experience, prefer and only conduct physical foreign currency exchange.
  • The main consideration is convenience (distance factor) and popularity

Feedback: The online system is easier to use than expected, but there are still many confusing signage issues and the system's idle time of only 10 minutes is a bit unfriendly to those who are slow to get started, and it does not record input information, which can be frustrating.

Suggestions: The system could be more personalized by displaying the necessary information such as the items to be brought to collect the foreign currency and the payment and collection time according to the different payment methods of the individual.

Interviewee 3 - Mr. Cai

  • 55-year-old male, a professional lawyer with a master's degree or above.
  • Lack of habit of using online banking.
  • Only had experience in currency exchange at the counter.

Feedback: Interviewee stated that if he could not quickly obtain information from Mega Bank's webpage, he would jump to other banks (commonly used banks) to search, and he might not continue to use Mega Bank to complete currency exchange.

Suggestions: The online currency exchange page should have a usage guide to make him understand what the next process would be. This is important information for designing the navigation page in the future

Interviewee 4 - Mr. Huang

  • 20-year-old male, a college student.
  • Internet banking experience. The purpose of online exchange is mainly for travel
  • Main consideration are: handling fee, availability of branches near the school, and handling time.
  • Also used other platforms that can transfer money across countries, mainly because ui/ux has sufficient feedback, intuitive and convenient operation.

Feedback: I felt that they would be willing to share the information with friends and relatives because of the next-day pickup, the free fee, and the ease of operation of MegaBank.

Suggestions: However, the screen was too simple and did not provide any feedback, and suggested that the design of wise would guide users better and make the experience more comfortable.
